All About Ruth

Boricua Asta La Muerte

Hello, I am Ruth and a Junior at Elon University this semester I hope to pass all my classes. I have meet many other aspiring Journalist's who motivate me to work hard. I am a transfer student from Guilford Technical Community College and graduated with an Associates in English. My major is Journalism with a minor in Literature within the School of Communications.


I am planning on doing an internship this summer close to home around Greensboro, North Carolina. It is difficult to find local internships that accept students with limited experience. I work as a reporter for the Elon News Network and hope to become an editor on the staff. In high school, I worked on the yearbook and newspaper to improve my writing skills before college. Looking to the future I want to work for "The New York Times" or "The Washingtin Post."


I was born and raised in High Point, North Carolina. I am proud to be Latina and a first generation student. As a Boricua, I love to show off the Puerto Rican flag. It's in my blood, heart, and life. I hope to inspire other Latinas to chase their dreams and to rise above the challenges. I grew up with four brothers and one sister so my house is always loud. My dogs Bella and Leo love to play outside, eat my food, and make a mess in my room.


Michelle and Jasper are my best friends who always support me. My hobbies include reading, writing, singing, dancing, sleeping, and excercising. My family is Pentacostal so I was raised in a religious home that reflect my values and beliefs in life. La Shekinah De Jehovah is a friendly, spanish, and small church in North Carolina. In the future, I wish to travel and learn about other cultures.
